# Interview Questions & Answers on Agentic AI

This page provides concise, interview‑ready answers to common questions about Agentic AI and its relationship to Generative AI.  
Use these as quick references for campus interviews or professional discussions.

## Q1: What is the difference between Generative AI and Agentic AI?

**Answer (Interview‑ready):**

Generative AI focuses on **creating new content** such as text, images, code, or music based on prompts. It relies on large language models (LLMs) to 
generate outputs in a one‑shot or chained manner.  
Agentic AI extends these capabilities by acting as an **autonomous agent**. It uses LLMs as the “brain” but goes 
further—**reasoning, planning, using tools, and executing actions** in real systems to achieve higher‑level goals.  
In short:  
- Generative AI → *Content creation*  
- Agentic AI → *Goal‑oriented orchestration and action*  

**Example:**  
Generative AI can write marketing copy. Agentic AI can deploy that copy, track campaign performance, and adjust the strategy automatically.

## Q2: Why are agents important in modern AI systems?

**Answer (Interview‑ready):**

Agents matter because they bring **autonomy and adaptability**. Instead of just producing outputs, they can:  
- Integrate with APIs and cloud services.  
- Automate workflows end‑to‑end.  
- Learn from feedback and improve over time.  

This makes them highly relevant for industry applications like customer support, workflow automation, and intelligent data analysis.

## Q3: How is Agentic AI relevant for interviews and industry?

**Answer (Interview‑ready):**

For interviews, knowing Agentic AI shows awareness of **cutting‑edge AI trends** beyond traditional ML. It helps candidates stand out by 
discussing autonomy, reasoning loops, and cloud integration.  
For industry, Agentic AI is already being applied in **Google Cloud (Vertex AI Agents, BigQuery workflows)** and other platforms to build scalable, 
intelligent systems. Professionals who understand agentic concepts can quickly adapt to these emerging tools and stay competitive.

## Q4: What are the core components of an agent in Agentic AI?
**Answer:**  
- **Perception**: Gather input (data, queries, environment signals).  
- **Reasoning**: Analyze context, set goals, plan steps.  
- **Action**: Execute tasks, call APIs, interact with tools.  
- **Reflection**: Evaluate outcomes, learn from feedback, adapt.  
This loop differentiates agents from static ML models.

## Q5: How does Agentic AI differ from traditional ML pipelines?
**Answer:**  
Traditional ML pipelines are model‑centric: data → training → prediction.  
Agentic AI is workflow‑centric: perception → reasoning → action → reflection.  
It emphasizes autonomy, tool use, and continuous adaptation rather than one‑shot predictions.
